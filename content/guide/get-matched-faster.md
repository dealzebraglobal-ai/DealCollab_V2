# Get Matched Faster — How to Answer the Intake Chat

**Last updated: [19/05/2026]**

The intake assistant structures your mandate for the matching engine. It asks
few questions — 2 to 4 at a time — and every answer directly shapes who you
get matched with. Here's how to get through it in two or three messages.

## The golden rule: ranges and descriptors, never names

You never need to share the company name, client identity, or any sensitive
detail. "Pune-based diagnostics chain, ₹30–40 Cr revenue, NABL accredited"
is a perfect answer. The assistant will remind you of this once — take it
seriously in both directions: don't over-share, and don't withhold the
non-sensitive basics that matching runs on.

## Your first message: the 30-second version

The more of these you put in your **first message**, the fewer questions you
face. Cover:

1. **What you're doing** — buying, selling, raising funds, seeking debt, or a
   partnership.
2. **Who you are** — the owner/promoter, or an advisor representing a client.
3. **What the business does** — sector and one line of description.
4. **Where** — city, state, or region (or target geography if buying).
5. **Scale** — revenue range and rough EBITDA (selling/raising), or budget
   range (buying).
6. **Structure** — full sale, majority, minority, or open.

**Example (sell-side):**
> "I'm an advisor representing a promoter selling a Nashik-based specialty
> chemicals manufacturer. Revenue ₹60–70 Cr, EBITDA ~₹9 Cr, mostly export.
> Full sale or majority stake."

**Example (buy-side):**
> "I'm the acquirer. Looking to acquire an NBFC with a clean RBI licence,
> anywhere in West India, ticket size ₹15–25 Cr, majority or 100%."

A message like that skips most of the questionnaire entirely.

## Have a teaser or brief? Paste it.

If you have a prepared teaser, IM summary, or structured brief, paste the
whole thing (with names redacted if you prefer). The assistant extracts
everything, shows you a summary, and asks you to confirm — one message
instead of a Q&A.

## The sector questions — why they matter

After the basics, the assistant asks 2–3 questions specific to your sector:
regulatory approvals for pharma, retainer mix for agencies, PPA profile for
energy assets, loan book quality for NBFCs. These aren't bureaucracy — they
are exactly what serious counterparties filter on. One specific answer here
("USFDA-approved plant, 12 DMFs filed") is worth more to your match quality
than everything else combined.

## Answer with what you have

- Exact numbers not required — ranges are fine ("revenue between ₹40–50 Cr").
- Don't know something? Say "not sure" and move on. An approximate mandate
  that gets submitted beats a perfect mandate that gets abandoned.
- Made an error? Just say so in chat — "correction, it's Gujarat, not
  Maharashtra" — the mandate updates.

## What happens after

Once the mandate has enough to match on, you'll confirm it's a genuine
requirement, and the engine takes over — evaluating your mandate against the
network continuously. Matches appear on your Deal Dashboard with the
reasoning behind each one. Everything from there is covered in **How
DealCollab Works**.

## Three mistakes that slow you down

1. **One-word answers dripped over ten messages.** The assistant groups
   questions so you can answer in bulk — do that.
2. **Withholding revenue/size entirely.** A range is anonymous and is the
   single strongest matching signal. No scale, weak matches.
3. **Vague sector descriptions.** "Manufacturing business" matches poorly;
   "auto-component machining, IATF certified, OEM supplier" matches well.
